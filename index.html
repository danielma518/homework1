Graph 1

Introduction

A chart showing the sales volume of Mac products in 2005 in terms of quarters.

Why is this visualisation problematic/ confusing/ misleading?

The baseline line of the Y-axis is 1000, making the change looks more drastic than it should be. Also, as it is a 3D chart, the closer the bars are, the bigger they look, which creates an illusion.

Further illustrations & calculations

If we create a line graph with an Y-axis that starts with 0 with the same set of data, it would be evident that the growth is just very mild. So we can conclude the 3D design and the Y-axis are two big factors that makes the chart misleading.

Suggestions

  1. Ues a 2D design instead of 3D to avoid illusion
  2. Adjust the Y-axis to show the actual growth
  3. Use a wider and flatter frame to make the chart
