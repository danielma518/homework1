Graph 1

Introduction

A chart showing the sales volume of Mac products in 2005 in terms of quarters.

Why is this visualisation problematic/ confusing/ misleading?

The baseline line of the Y-axis is 1000, making the change looks more drastic than it should be. Also, as it is a 3D chart, the closer the bars are, the bigger they look, which creates an illusion.

Further illustrations & calculations

If we create a line graph with an Y-axis that starts with 0 with the same set of data, it would be evident that the growth is just very mild. So we can conclude the 3D design and the Y-axis are two big factors that makes the chart misleading.

Suggestions

  1. Ues a 2D design instead of 3D to avoid illusion
  2. Adjust the Y-axis to show the actual growth
  3. Use a wider and flatter frame to make the chart

Graph 2

Introduction

A graph showing the change of median income for US men in 2012 in terms of age group.

Why is this visualisation problematic/ confusing/ misleading?

The baseline line of the Y-axis is $32000 instead of $0, making the drop in income looks more drastic than it has been. Also, as there are only 2 years on the X-axis, it results in only 2 straight lines in the graph, which cannot reflectively show how the income has really changed in the past 40 years.

Further illustrations & calculations

If we recreate a line graph with all the 40 years on the X-axis with the same set of data, it would show much more reflective change of the income throughout the years, including a mild increase in around 1980-1983, and some other changes that cannot be shown in the above chart.

Suggestions

  1. Adjust the Y-axis by starting from 0 in order to show the actual growth accurately.
  2. Add more years to the X-axis in order to show the change of income, which is more sophistiacted than it appears in the graph above, more detailedly.
